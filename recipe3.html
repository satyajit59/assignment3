Chicken Curry

Prep Time: 1 hour Difficulty: hard

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken
  • 2 cloves, Garlic, ginger
  • peas and chicken broth
  • coriander, onion and curry powder
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder, 1.5 tsp red chili powder, 1.5 tsp coriander powder, 1 tsp garam masala, and salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add whole spices and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté on medium heat until they turn deep golden brown (about 8–10 minutes).
  2. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 1–2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the tomatoes, turmeric, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy and the oil starts to separate from the sides.
  4. Lower the heat and add red chili powder, coriander powder, and half of the garam masala. Stir for a minute to bloom the spices.
  5. Add the chicken pieces. Increase heat to medium-high and fry (bhuno) for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken turns pale and is well-coated with the masala.
  6. Pour in 1 cup of hot water (using hot water prevents the meat from toughening). Cover and simmer on low-medium heat for 20–25 minutes until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  7. Sprinkle the remaining garam masala and chopped coriander leaves. Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.
